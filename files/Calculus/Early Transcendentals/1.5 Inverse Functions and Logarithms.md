**Inverse Functions**
<hr style="margin-top: 8px" />

The **number of bacteria** $N$ is a function of **time** $t$:
$$
N = f(t)
$$
The **inverse function** of $f$ would be:
$$
t = f^{-1}(N)
$$
Only **one-to-one functions** can have inverse functions

> **(1) Definition**: A function $f$ is called **one-to-one** if it never takes on the same value twice
> $f(x_1) \neq f(x_2) \text{ whenever } x_1 \neq x_2$

> **Horizontal Line Test**: A function is one-to-one iff no horizontal line intersects it's graph more than once

> **(2) Definition**: Let $f$ be a function with domain $A$ and range $B$. Then it's inverse function $f^{-1}$ has domain $B$ and range $A$ and is defined by
> $f^{-1}(y) = x \Leftrightarrow f(x) = y$

- domain of $f^{-1}$ = range of $f$
- range of $f^{-1}$ = domain of $f$

**Cancellation equations**:
> $f^{-1}(f(x)) = x$ for every $x$ in $A$
> $f(f^{-1}(x)) = x$ for every $x$ in $B$

Finding the inverse function of a one-to-one function $f$:
1. Write $y = f(x)$
2. Solve this equation for $x$ in terms of $y$
3. To express $f^{-1}$ as a function of $x$, interchange $x$ and $y$.

The graph of an inverse function $f^{-1}$ is obtained by reflecting $f$ about the line $y = x$

**Logarithmic Functions**
<hr style="margin-top: 8px" />

$f(x) = b^x$:
- Called the **exponential function**
- Is either increasing or decreasing & therefore is one-to-one
- It's inverse is the **logarithmic function with base $b$**

> $^blog(x) = y \Leftrightarrow b^y = x$

**Cancellation equations:**
> $^blog(b^x) = x$ for every $x \in \mathbb{R}$
> $b^{^blog(x)} = x$ for every $x > 0$

**Laws of Logarithms**: If $x$ and $y$ are positive numbers, then
1. $log(x) + log(y) = log(xy)$
2. $log(x) - log(y) = log(\dfrac{x}{y})$
3. $r \cdot log(x) = log(x^r)$

<hr style="margin-bottom: 8px" />
**Natural Logarithms**
<hr style="margin-top: 8px" />

The logarithm with base $e$ is called the **natural logarithm** $ln(x)$

**Cancellation equations:**
> $ln(e^x) = x$ for every $x \in \mathbb{R}$
> $e^{ln(x)} = x$ for every $x > 0$

A power of $x$ can be expressed in an equivalent exponential form:
>$x^r = e^{r \cdot ln(x)}$

**Change of Base Formula:**
$$
log_b(x) = \dfrac{ln(x)}{ln(b)}
$$

**Inverse Trigonometric Functions**
<hr style="margin-top: 8px" />

Trigonometric functions become one-to-one when restricting the domain

$$
sin^{-1}(x) = y \Leftrightarrow sin(y) = x \text{ and } - \dfrac{\pi}{2} \leq y \leq \dfrac{\pi}{2}
$$
**Cancellation Equations**:
> $sin^{-1}(sin(x)) = x$ for $- \dfrac{\pi}{2} \leq x \leq \dfrac{\pi}{2}$
> $sin(sin^{-1}(x)) = x$ for $-1 \leq x \leq 1$
